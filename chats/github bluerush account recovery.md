## Recovering or reclaiming the @bluerush account

Don't worry — GitHub has defined recovery and unlinking paths for accounts you don't control; here's what you can do next.

Steps you can take now

1. Try automated account recovery (if you can access a recovery factor)

- If you can trigger a password reset for any email that might be the account's primary or backup, follow the flow at [https://github.com/password_reset (opens in a new tab)](https://github.com/password_reset). During the reset flow you can choose **Begin account or email recovery** under “More options” and pick a recovery factor you previously set (verified device, SSH key, or personal access token). See **Recovering your account if you lose your 2FA credentials** in GitHub Docs: **[https://docs.github.com/en/authentication/securing-your-account-with-two-factor-authentication-2fa/recovering-your-account-if-you-lose-your-2fa-credentials (opens in a new tab)](https://docs.github.com/en/authentication/securing-your-account-with-two-factor-authentication-2fa/recovering-your-account-if-you-lose-your-2fa-credentials)**.

2. If you cannot use any recovery method and the account is locked with 2FA

- Per GitHub’s Account Recovery Policy, GitHub Support cannot restore access to accounts with two-factor authentication enabled if the recovery methods are lost. If you truly cannot use any of the documented recovery methods, the account is permanently inaccessible. See: **GitHub Account Recovery Policy** — **[https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-account-recovery-policy (opens in a new tab)](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-account-recovery-policy)**.

3. Unlink the email from the locked account so you can reuse it

- If the @bluerush account is 2FA-locked and you cannot recover it, you can request unlinking of an email address from the locked account so that the email becomes available to create or attach to a new account. Unlinking is only available for accounts with 2FA enabled. Follow the documentation referenced under “Unlinking your email address from a locked account” in the Account Recovery Policy: **[https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-account-recovery-policy (opens in a new tab)](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-account-recovery-policy)** (the Policy links to the how-to page for unlinking).

4. What GitHub Support can and cannot do

- GitHub Support will not restore access to a 2FA-locked account without valid recovery methods and does not accept social or ID verification to regain access. Support can assist with removing a payment method from a locked account or processing an unlink request for an email address from a locked account, but they will not transfer repositories or restore account contents. These limitations are documented in the Account Recovery Policy: **[https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-account-recovery-policy (opens in a new tab)](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-account-recovery-policy)**.
